Beauty and Sustainability

Working to keep Homewood beautiful - a partnership between city and nature..

The Homewood Environmental Commission was established to promote and provide for active and continuing public education programs about the environmental health of our community.

Promote

The HEC cares about our community. With programs like our residential tree planting, recycling education, and more, we work to improve and protect the environmental beauty of our city.

Homewood residents can promote environmental issues by participating in local events, spreading the word, contacting department heads and your councilors, and making sustainable choices in their daily lives. This can include reducing waste, using public transportation, supporting local businesses that prioritize sustainability, and advocating for environmentally-friendly policies in the community. By working together, Homewood residents can make a positive impact on the environment and promote a healthier, more sustainable community for all. more. Or maybe you have a creative project to share with the world. You can also receive our Newsletter to stay up to date with what’s currently going on with the Homewood Environmental Committee.

    Ways You Can Help


  • Contact local government officials to voice concerns and support initiatives that promote sustainability and protect the environment.
  • Implement recycling habits by sorting materials properly and reducing waste through composting.
  • Use energy-efficient appliances and light bulbs, and turn off electronics when not in use to conserve energy.
  • Reduce water usage by taking shorter showers, fixing leaks, and using drought-resistant plants in landscaping.
  • Use alternative modes of transportation such as biking or walking, carpooling, or using public transit.
  • Plant trees and native plants to improve air quality and reduce carbon emissions.
  • Shop locally and support sustainable businesses that prioritize environmental practices.
  • Reduce single-use plastic consumption by using reusable bags, bottles, and containers.
  • Participate in community cleanup events and volunteer for environmental organizations.
  • Educate others on the importance of environmental sustainability and encourage sustainable practices.

Recycling

Recycling is an important environmental issue that can help reduce waste, conserve resources, and protect the planet. Despite its benefits, many communities still struggle with low recycling rates and contamination issues. According to the EPA, in 2018, only 32.1% of the total waste generated in the United States was recycled, while the rest ended up in landfills or incinerators. This highlights the need for increased education and awareness about recycling practices and the impact of waste on the environment. Recycling also plays a role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions, as it requires less energy to recycle materials than to extract new ones. Additionally, recycling can create jobs and support local economies through the development of recycling facilities and the sale of recycled materials.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) - Homewood Environmental Commission

General Questions

What is the role of the Homewood Environmental Commission?

The Homewood Environmental Commission promotes sustainable practices, preserves natural resources, and raises environmental awareness in the community. We work to keep Homewood beautiful through a partnership between the city and nature.

How can I get involved?

Attend meetings (3rd Tuesday each month), volunteer for events, or join subcommittees. Stay updated through the website and social media.

Are there educational programs or workshops?

Yes. We host programs on recycling, energy conservation, tree preservation, sustainable gardening, and more. See the Residents page for updates.

Do you provide sustainability resources for residents?

Yes. We offer guidance on recycling, energy-saving, water conservation, composting, and eco-friendly practices through our site and meetings.

Do you collaborate with local schools?

Yes, including partnerships with Samford University. We provide presentations, workshops, and student engagement opportunities.

How do I report environmental concerns?

Contact City Hall at 205-332-6800 or reach out to your city councilor. Providing details helps resolve issues faster.

Do you offer grants or funding?

We do not directly offer funding, but we encourage residents and organizations to explore opportunities and bring ideas to city leadership.

How can I stay updated?

Visit the website regularly and follow us on Facebook and Instagram. You can also join our mailing list.


Recycling & Trash

What are the recycling basics?

Please bag garbage and small recyclables before placing them in the cart.

What CAN I recycle?

Household garbage, recyclables, yard debris, fertilizers, broken-down boxes, batteries, bulbs, and packing materials (bagged).

What can NOT be recycled?

Construction debris, concrete, dirt piles, tires, hazardous waste, car batteries, large auto parts, railroad ties, medical waste, and animal carcasses.
