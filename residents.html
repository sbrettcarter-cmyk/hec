City Council

Homewood is divided into 5 wards with 2 representatives each, plus a council president and mayor. Officials are elected every four years.

Council meetings are held on the 2nd and 4th Monday of each month at City Hall.

Recycling

Recycling reduces waste, conserves resources, and lowers emissions. However, contamination and low participation remain challenges.

Recycling also supports local economies and reduces energy use compared to producing new materials.

Parks

Parks promote physical activity, mental well-being, and environmental sustainability.

  • Encourage exercise and healthy lifestyles
  • Provide access to nature
  • Strengthen community connections
  • Support biodiversity and climate resilience

The Power of Trees

Trees improve air quality, reduce heat, manage water, and increase property values. Homewood is recognized as a Tree City USA.

  • Improve air quality
  • Reduce urban heat
  • Prevent erosion
  • Boost local economy

A tree ordinance protects trees during construction and requires permits for removal.
